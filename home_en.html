What a music publisher does

A music publisher deals in musical works and the copyrights attached to those musical works. We therefore do not deal in the recordings themselves, but in the copyrights that are attached to the recordings.

Now you might think, isnt that what a record label does? But the answer to that is no. A music publisher concludes publishing contracts with the composers and lyricists, they are the creators of the music.
A record label concludes contracts with artists and bands, they are the performing musicians. The record company deals in, among other things, the neighboring rights attached to the recordings themselves. They also guide and promote the artists and recordings.
